Feature Specifications

A detailed breakdown of all 8 features — what each one does, how it works technically, and what it delivers commercially.

1
Leaderboards — Top Supporters
€1,400

A ranked list displayed on each creator's profile showing their highest-spending fans — commonly known as "whales." This is a proven retention and monetisation mechanic used by platforms like Twitch and gaming apps — high-spending fans compete to hold their spot, which directly increases average spend per session.

  • Leaderboard ranks fans by total lifetime spend on that creator (tips + subscriptions + PPV + marketplace)
  • Creator controls visibility — public leaderboard or private (fans only see their own rank)
  • Top 3 fans highlighted with special badge treatment (Gold, Silver, Bronze)
  • Real-time updates via Socket.IO — rank changes instantly when a tip or purchase is made
  • Fan profile shows their rank on every creator they support
  • Optional: Creator can set custom rewards or shoutouts for top ranked fans
  • Admin panel view — platform-wide top supporters across all creators
Prisma Aggregations Socket.IO Real-time React UI Existing wallet data — no new DB cost
2
Crowdfunding Goals
€1,600

A visual progress bar on the creator's profile page. The creator sets a goal (title + target amount + optional deadline). Every tip or contribution moves the bar forward in real-time. This creates a direct emotional connection between fan spending and creator outcomes — proven to increase average tip size.

  • Creator dashboard: create, edit, and close goals from settings
  • Multiple concurrent goals supported (e.g. "New Camera" and "Studio Rent")
  • Progress bar updates live via Socket.IO the moment a fan tips or contributes
  • Goal completion triggers a celebration animation on the creator's profile
  • Creator can send an automated thank-you notification when goal is reached
  • Expired goals (past deadline, not reached) archived — creator can mark as failed or extend
  • Goal history visible to creator in dashboard — useful for showing fans past successes
New Prisma Model BullMQ deadline worker Socket.IO progress push
3
AI Smart-Pricing for PPV
€1,500

When a creator sets a price on a PPV post or paid message, the AI analyses the intended recipient fan's behaviour — their total spend, frequency of purchases, subscription tier, and engagement level — and suggests an optimal price range. The creator always sets the final price; the AI guides the decision.

  • Creator sees a "Suggested price" callout when creating PPV content directed at a specific fan
  • AI analyses: fan's lifetime spend, last 30-day spend, average PPV purchase amount, tip frequency
  • Suggestions are fan-specific — a high-spending fan gets a higher suggestion than a new subscriber
  • Uses Anthropic Claude API (already integrated in the platform) — no new API setup
  • Creator can dismiss suggestion and set their own price — suggestion is never forced
  • Suggestion accuracy improves over time as more fan spending data accumulates
  • Admin panel: AI suggestion usage stats — how often creators use vs override suggestions
Anthropic Claude API Existing spend history data No new third-party API key needed
4
Auto-Captioning & Translation
€2,500

Automatically generates subtitles for uploaded videos and translates them into 10+ languages. This removes the language barrier for creators who want to grow globally without hiring translators. A creator posting in English can instantly reach Spanish, French, German, Arabic, and Japanese audiences.

  • Uploaded video processed via AWS Transcribe — subtitle file (WebVTT) generated automatically
  • AWS Translate converts subtitles into 10 languages: EN, ES, FR, DE, AR, JA, PT, IT, NL, PL
  • Fan selects preferred language from video player — subtitles switch instantly
  • Creator dashboard shows transcription status (Processing / Ready / Failed) per video
  • Transcription job runs asynchronously via BullMQ — no upload delay for creator
  • Creator can edit auto-generated captions before publishing for accuracy
  • Subtitle files stored and served via existing Hetzner file storage
Third-Party Running Costs

AWS Transcribe: ~$0.024 per minute of video. AWS Translate: ~$15 per million characters. These are passed through at actual usage cost — not included in the development fee.

5
AI Viral Clip Generator
€3,500

After a live stream ends, the platform automatically analyses the full recording and identifies the most engaging moments — peak audience activity, audio spikes, exciting transcript segments. The creator is presented with suggested clips (30–90 seconds each) and can publish them as Stories or Teasers in one click.

  • Live streams are recorded to storage during broadcast (new infrastructure — does not exist today)
  • Post-stream: BullMQ triggers async analysis job — creator is not blocked
  • FFmpeg audio analysis detects high-energy moments (volume spikes, crowd reactions)
  • AWS Transcribe generates transcript — AI identifies key moments from content context
  • Top 5–10 candidate clips ranked and presented to creator with timestamps
  • Creator previews clips, selects which to publish — one-click post as Story or Teaser
  • Clips auto-cropped to vertical 9:16 format for Story compatibility
  • Creator can trim and rename clips before publishing
Note on Live Stream Recording

Recording infrastructure needs to be built as part of this feature. Additional Hetzner storage is required (~€5–15/month depending on volume). Detection accuracy is approximately 80% — clips are suggestions, not guaranteed viral moments. Creator has full editorial control.

6
Escrow Service for Physical Items
€1,800

Supporting "Direct Pay" for physical goods — when a fan purchases a physical item, funds are held in escrow on the platform rather than going directly to the creator. The creator ships the item. When the buyer confirms receipt — or after 14 days automatically — funds are released to the creator's wallet. Protects both buyer and seller on every physical transaction.

  • New marketplace listing type: "Physical Item" (alongside existing Digital listing)
  • On purchase: funds deducted from fan wallet and held in escrow (not credited to creator yet)
  • Creator notified to ship — can add tracking number to the order
  • Buyer receives "Confirm Receipt" button — click releases funds to creator immediately
  • Auto-release after 14 days if buyer does not respond (configurable in admin settings)
  • Dispute flow: buyer opens dispute within 14-day window — admin reviews and mediates
  • Admin panel: full escrow queue — pending, disputed, completed transactions
  • Platform fee (20%) applied at time of release, same as all other transactions
New Prisma Escrow model BullMQ auto-release job Admin dispute UI
7
In-Stream Shopping
€1,200

During a live stream, the creator can pin any of their active marketplace listings to the bottom of the stream overlay. All viewers see the item in real-time. Fans can tap "Buy Now" and complete the purchase via a modal — without leaving the stream. Impulse purchase conversion at its highest point of engagement.

  • Creator controls panel during stream: search and pin any of their marketplace listings
  • Pinned item appears as a persistent card at the bottom of all viewers' stream UI
  • Card shows: item image, title, price, and "Buy Now" button
  • Purchase modal opens over the stream (stream continues playing in background)
  • Checkout uses existing wallet balance — no redirect, no page change
  • Creator can unpin or swap item at any time during stream
  • Socket.IO pushes pin/unpin events to all viewers in real-time
Socket.IO stream events Existing marketplace + wallet No new third-party APIs
8
Dynamic Watermarking
€1,500

Every viewer sees a subtle watermark on video content bearing their own username or IP address. If a video is leaked outside the platform, the watermark identifies exactly who had access at that time. This is a strong deterrent against piracy and provides accountability for premium content.

  • Uploaded videos: FFmpeg post-processing embeds a semi-transparent username or IP watermark at render time
  • Live streams: Client-side canvas layer renders username or IP subtly over the video player
  • Watermark position rotates randomly — harder to crop out
  • Opacity and font size configurable by admin (default: subtle, non-intrusive)
  • Creator can enable or disable watermarking per content piece
  • Admin panel: flag reported leaked content and identify watermark source
Important Note

Client-side watermarking (used by OnlyFans, Fanvue, and all major adult platforms) can be bypassed by a secondary camera recording the screen. It is a proven deterrent — not a 100% foolproof forensic system. Server-side per-viewer stream encoding would require prohibitive CPU resources and is not industry-standard practice.
